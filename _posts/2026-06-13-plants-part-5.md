## Fall 2021
As mentioned in part 4, my plant collection was in poor shape after I came back from my summer abroad and I had to get rid of many plants. This made me sad and hence I don't have a ton of plant photos from this time. 

### Let's normalize dead plants
While many people know me as somewhat of a "plant wizard",<d-footnote>Not self-proclaimed---a friend of mine called me this!</d-footnote> by no means have I never killed any plants. Let this be a lesson for all of you, whether you're also a plant nerd or thinking of getting your first houseplant: killing some of your plants is inevitable and normal, and part of the learning process.

Let's pause for a second and commemorate some plants that I could not save in this time period. I rarely take pictures of plants that are not thriving<d-footnote>Although I'm trying to be better at documenting this in the future.</d-footnote>, but here are two:

[IMGS: brazil_alive_mar2021.png | Philodendron Brazil that was alive and well in March 2021. :: brazil_dying_oct2021.png | By October, all the vines died and I cut it back to basically nothing in an attempt to save it. :: brazil_dead_jan2022.png | January 2022, I had to accept it's death.]

[IMGS: spider_alive_apr2021.png | My curly spider plants were... :: spider2_alive_apr2021.png | ...alive and well in April 2021. ::  spiderplants_dead.png | September 2021, this was all that's left, and they both died.]

[IMGS (all portrait): melange_apr2021.png | April 2021, my "melange" of philodendrons I'd propagated during the pandemic. :: melange_jan2022.png | By January, it was very sickly and full of thrips. :: melange_death_jan2022.png | About a week later, I chucked it*. ]

*Actually, I chucked _most_ of it. I fished out 1-2 pieces of my particularly sentimental philodendron Micans to try and save---spoiler alert, it's still alive today! But that story is very long, and will absolutely get it's own blog post in the future...

### Krimson Princess

Not all was negative in this time period. I want to highlight a plant I got in September 2021: *hoya carnosa tricolor*, also known as the Krimson Princess. I got her from this little dépanneur, [Marché Milton Parc](https://m-miltonparc.com/), which I would frequent when I was feeling down. The minute I saw this beauty, I knew I needed to shell out the money to get her. This was probably the most expensive plant<d-footnote>This is actually a very common hoya and typically inexpensive. The reason this was mildly expensive was due to it's size.</d-footnote> I'd bought up until that point, and I was so excited I busted out my nice camera and did a photoshoot with this plant:

[IMGS: tricolor/pickup_sept2021.png (portrait) | In the plant store, September 2021. :: tricolor/wholeplant.png (portrait) :: marchemiltonparc.png (landscape, can be small) | Marché Milton Parc store.]
